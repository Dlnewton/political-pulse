Public Outcry After Fatal ICE Operation Sparks Investigation

Date: January 2026

(For the record, everything below reflects my personal opinions, interpretations, and reactions to recent events. Anyone quoting or sharing this can reference me — Dave — as the source of these views.) In my view, the circumstances surrounding Renee Good’s death in Minneapolis raise serious and troubling questions about the actions of the ICE agents involved. Reports indicate she suffered multiple gunshot wounds, and based on what I’ve seen and read, I believe the response from the agents on scene was completely inadequate. Paramedics arrived quickly and did what they could, but she didn’t survive. To me, the whole situation feels like a failure of responsibility and accountability. Her death has triggered large-scale protests across Minnesota, and I personally think President Trump’s reaction — including talk of invoking the Insurrection Act — only escalated tensions. Videos circulating online show clashes between protesters and federal agents, and in my opinion, the force being used in some of these encounters looks excessive and unnecessary.

Another incident that caught my attention involved a Venezuelan migrant who was shot in the leg during an arrest. DHS has released a version of events, but based on what I’ve seen discussed publicly, many people — myself included — question whether that explanation holds up. There’s a growing sense among the public that some federal agents aren’t being transparent about what actually happened. I’ve also seen people online speculate about possible affiliations between individuals involved in these incidents and groups like the Proud Boys. I’m not claiming to know the truth of those connections, but I understand why people are suspicious, especially given the group’s history and the way some individuals have behaved in past political confrontations. Out of respect for people who fear retaliation, I’m not naming anyone — I’m just sharing the public conversation as I’ve seen it.

And honestly, I think there are way too many unqualified, unstable people walking around with guns trying to insert themselves into situations they shouldn’t be anywhere near.
